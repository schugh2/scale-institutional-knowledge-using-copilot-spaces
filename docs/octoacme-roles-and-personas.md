# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and continuously improve team processes. They enable the team to deliver predictably by focusing on process health and team dynamics.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Monitor team velocity and sprint health
- Identify and escalate impediments blocking progress
- Coach the team on Agile principles and practices
- Maintain and optimize the project board and workflow
- Support estimation and capacity planning

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Reduce process friction and unplanned work

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members when addressing blockers
- Metrics reports (velocity, burndown, cycle time)

---

## Quality Assurance / QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality standards, design test strategies, and ensure acceptance criteria are met. They advocate for quality throughout the delivery lifecycle and partner with developers to prevent defects.

### Responsibilities
- Design test plans and acceptance criteria validation approaches
- Lead manual QA testing for complex features and regression suites
- Collaborate with developers on automated test coverage and CI integration
- Identify and triage bugs, validate fixes
- Report quality metrics and test coverage
- Advise on quality trade-offs and release readiness

### Goals
- Minimize production defects and rework
- Increase test automation and reduce manual effort
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release

### Typical Communication
- Test case and acceptance criteria review with developers
- QA status updates in weekly syncs
- Bug reports and release readiness assessments

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects define system design, scalability, and technical direction. They ensure solutions align with long-term vision and organizational standards while mitigating technical risk.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture and integration points
- Review technical proposals and provide design guidance
- Identify scalability, security, and performance risks
- Mentor developers on architectural patterns and best practices
- Support capacity and infrastructure planning
- Guide technology selection and tool evaluation

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, secure systems
- Reduce technical debt and rework
- Accelerate delivery through reusable patterns and guidance

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings with engineering teams
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical mentoring and code review feedback

---

## Executive Sponsor / Stakeholder Champion

### Role Summary
Executive Sponsors provide business context, secure resources, and remove organizational barriers. They champion the project at the leadership level and ensure alignment with strategic goals.

### Responsibilities
- Secure budget, staffing, and organizational resources
- Remove cross-team and organizational blockers
- Align project with business strategy and priorities
- Provide executive visibility and stakeholder updates
- Make go/no-go decisions at key milestones
- Escalate business impact and outcomes

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers measurable business value
- Maintain leadership alignment and support
- Minimize organizational friction and delays

### Typical Communication
- Monthly/milestone stakeholder briefings
- Executive decision forums
- Risk and dependency escalations

---

## Support / Customer Success Lead

### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success representatives advocate for customer needs, escalate issues, and ensure smooth adoption post-release. They bridge the gap between engineering and customer experience.

### Responsibilities
- Advocate for customer pain points and usability needs
- Support user acceptance testing and beta feedback
- Plan and execute customer training and onboarding
- Triage customer-reported issues and feedback
- Provide metrics on adoption, satisfaction, and support burden
- Coordinate post-release support and incident response

### Goals
- Maximize customer adoption and satisfaction
- Reduce support burden through better product design
- Capture customer feedback for product improvements

### Typical Communication
- Feature acceptance testing and feedback sessions
- Customer issue triage and escalation
- Adoption and support metrics in stakeholder updates
